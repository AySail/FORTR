About the Solar System

Our Solar System consists of the Sun and everything bound to it by gravity â€” the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, comets, asteroids, and countless particles of dust and gas. It formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a giant rotating cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula. The planets orbit the Sun in nearly circular paths and are divided into terrestrial planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and gas and ice giants (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune). Exploring these planets helps us understand the history of our cosmic neighborhood and the formation of planetary systems throughout the universe. No pluto here only plants sorry!
