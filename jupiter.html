Welcome to the Gas Giant: Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its powerful storms and thick atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. It has a vast system of moons and rings.

Fun Facts About Jupiter

  • Jupiter is more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined.
  • The Great Red Spot is a giant storm larger than Earth that has lasted for centuries.
  • It has at least 79 moons, including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
  • Jupiter's magnetic field is the strongest of any planet in the solar system.

Ongoing Jupiter Research

Missions like Juno are studying Jupiter’s atmosphere, magnetic field, and interior to better understand gas giant planets.

Why Study Jupiter?

Understanding Jupiter sheds light on planetary formation and the dynamics of giant planets, which are common in the universe.

Human Impact on Jupiter

While humans can't visit Jupiter directly, robotic explorers provide invaluable insights into this massive planet’s environment.
