Welcome to the Smallest Planet: Mercury

Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, is a rocky world with extreme temperatures and a very thin atmosphere. Its swift orbit and cratered surface reveal much about the early solar system.

Fun Facts About Mercury

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system and has a diameter of about 4,880 km.
  • It experiences extreme temperature swings, from about -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.
  • Mercury has a very thin atmosphere composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen.
  • The planet has a heavily cratered surface, similar in appearance to our Moon.

Ongoing Mercury Research

Space missions continue to study Mercury's magnetic field, geology, and exosphere to understand planet formation and solar influences.

Why Study Mercury?

Studying Mercury helps scientists understand the conditions of the early solar system and the effects of solar radiation on rocky planets.

Human Impact on Mercury

Though Mercury is inaccessible for humans, robotic missions expand our knowledge of this extreme world closest to the Sun.
