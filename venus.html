Welcome to the Veiled World: Venus

Step into the mysterious and scorching world of Venus, shrouded by thick clouds of sulfuric acid and intense heat. As Earth’s closest neighbor, Venus holds many secrets about planetary evolution and extreme atmospheres. Join us as we explore its harsh environment and intriguing characteristics.

Fun Facts About Venus

  • Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is similar in size to Earth.
  • Its thick atmosphere is mostly carbon dioxide, causing a runaway greenhouse effect.
  • Surface temperatures reach up to 465°C (869°F), hot enough to melt lead.
  • Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets — a day on Venus is longer than its year.

Ongoing Venus Research

Scientists use orbiters and planned missions to study Venus’s atmosphere, geology, and potential volcanic activity to better understand planetary climate and evolution.

Why Study Venus?

Venus provides a natural laboratory to study greenhouse gas effects and climate extremes, offering insights that can help us better understand Earth’s climate system.

Human Impact on Venus

Though far from human reach, Venus inspires caution and curiosity about climate change and planetary stewardship here on Earth.

Clouds and Atmosphere

The thick clouds of sulfuric acid hide Venus’s surface and create spectacular light scattering, making it the brightest planet in our night sky.

Latest News

New missions like NASA’s VERITAS and ESA’s EnVision aim to unlock Venus’s mysteries in the coming decade with advanced radar and atmospheric instruments.
